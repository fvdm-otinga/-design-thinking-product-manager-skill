## Empathy Map Template

For each persona, map four quadrants:

```markdown
### Empathy Map: [Persona Name]

**THINKS** (internal thoughts, beliefs, concerns)
- What occupies their mind regarding this problem?
- What worries them?
- What assumptions do they hold?
- What do they aspire to?

**FEELS** (emotions, emotional drivers)
- What frustrates them?
- What excites or motivates them?
- What fears do they have?
- What gives them confidence?

**SAYS** (verbalized statements, language used)
- How do they describe the problem to others?
- What do they complain about?
- What do they ask for explicitly?
- What language/jargon do they use?

**DOES** (observable actions, behaviors)
- What steps do they take today?
- What workarounds do they use?
- How do they interact with existing tools?
- What do they avoid doing (and why)?
```

**Empathy map quality checklist:**
- Contradictions between quadrants are noted (says one thing, does another)
- Emotional drivers are identified, not just surface-level feelings
- Observable behaviors are grounded in real research or validated assumptions
- Language captures their vocabulary, not the PM's vocabulary

## Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD) Framework

Capture the jobs users are hiring a solution to do:

```markdown
### Job Statement Format
"When I [situation/context], I want to [motivation/force], so I can [desired outcome]."

### Functional Jobs (practical tasks to accomplish)
1. [Job statement]
   - Current solution:
   - Satisfaction level (1-5):
   - Frequency:

### Emotional Jobs (how they want to feel)
1. [Job statement]
   - Current emotional state:
   - Desired emotional state:

### Social Jobs (how they want to be perceived)
1. [Job statement]
   - Current perception:
   - Desired perception:

### Related Jobs (adjacent tasks in the workflow)
1. [Job statement]
   - Relationship to core job:
```

**JTBD quality checklist:**
- Jobs are solution-agnostic (describe the need, not a feature)
- Jobs include context/situation triggers
- Functional, emotional, and social dimensions are covered
- Jobs are prioritized by importance and current satisfaction gap
- Over-served jobs identified (don't compete here)
- Under-served jobs identified (opportunity space)
